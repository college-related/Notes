Management

  • Management is the process of managing all resources ( human and non-human ) in such a way that it gives complete satisfaction to human being.

Concept of management

  • Functional Concept : The process of managing, coordinating, motivating and controlling efforts of others. What a manager does.
  • Human Relation Concept : The process of creating environment where indivdual could cooperate towards attaining of group goals.
  • Leadership and Decision Making Concept : The process of preparing, organizing, directing human efforts for the benifits of men.
  • Productive Concept : The process of securing maximum prosperity with minimum effort.
  • Integration Concept : The process of co-ordination of human and material resources towards the achievement of organizational goal.

Functions of Management

  • Planning : Forecasting the future as accurately as possible and planinng for the project such as what to do, how to do, etc
  • Organizing : Distributing the authority and responsibilities in the organization
  • Staffing : Maintaning the right people, at right time, at right place to attain their duties.
  • Leading : Taking decision and motivating employee to lead the organization
  • Controlling : Checking/Auditing the movement of organization towards the goal based on the planning made.
  • POSLC : [Planning - Organizing - Staffing - Leading - Controlling ]

Properties of Management

  • Division of labor : There should be a distribution of work among the organization in order to have a high productivity.
  • Authority and Responsibility : There should be balance of authority and responsibility.
  • Scalar Chain : For better communication the heirarchy of levels should be scalar (less)
  • Unity of Command : Commanding/Assiging must be done in as singular way as possible to avoid confusion.
  • Unity of Direction : One must work at a single goal at a time and towards the organizational goal.
  • Centralization : Although decentralization of power is needed at levels like ( regular decision making, etc ) but the final decision making for the organizational goal must be at the top or center of the heirarchy.
  • Equity : Every one on the organization must be treated equally.
  • Order : Right people, at right place at right time
  • Initiative : Everyone should be given opportunity to show their initiative.
  • Discipline : Everyone must be discipline and fulfill their repsonsibility and make good use of their authority.
  • Remmumeration : Employee must be given rewards in a logical and effective way in order to have motivated employees.
  • Stability of Tenure : Turnover of employee must be kept less in order to minimize the cost of organization.
  • Subordination of individual interest to general interest : Organization must make one’s interest to organizational interest.
  • Esprit De Corps (Unity is Strength) : Every individual must be united to the goal of the organization.

Importance of Management

  1. Helps in achieving group goals.
  2. Optimal uitilization of resources
  3. Reduces cost
  4. Establishes Sound organization
  5. Establishes euqilibrium
  6. Establishes prosperity to society

Roles of Manager

  1. Interpersonal Role
    • FigureHead: Performing ceremonical and symbolic acts as the formal representative of the organization
    • Leader: Encouraging and motivating employee creating a productive atmosphere/environment in the workplace.
    • Liasion: Networking with peers outside the organization.
  2. Informational Role
    • Monitor: Managing information comming from both formal and informal channels.
    • Disseminator: Passing the information to the members of their staff who otherwise wouldn’t have access to it.
    • Spokes Person: Sending information through speech, letters, memos, reports, etc to people outside the department or organization
  3. Decisional Role
    • Enterprenure: Initiating changes within the organization
    • Disturbance handler: Reacting to changes and conditions beyond their control
    • Resource allocator: Distributing resources to achieve the organizational goal
    • Negotiator: Bargaining with unions, Contracting with suppliers, etc

Managment Skills

  • Traditional Concept : In this model only the Top level manager and middle suppervisor level must have management skill
  • Contemporary Concept : In this model all the levels of the organization must have management skill only difference is the amount of knowlege they must have for each different skill set.

Management styles

  1. Autocratic Every decision is made by top levels only.
  2. Bureaucratic Every decision is made according to rules and regulations
  3. Democratic Initiation is done by manager and everyone is involved in the decision making.
  4. Laissez - Fair (Delegative) All decision is done by employees and manager only intervene when needed.
  5. Charismatic Manager takes the final decision and uses his perssuasive skill to convince the employee.

Organization

  • It is a group of people with similar or common interest gathering together in order to achieve a common objective or goal.

Concept of organization

  1. Static Concept
    • In this concept, organization is the group of people bound together formally to achieve common objective.
    • It lays emphasis on position and not individual.
  2. Dynamic Concept
    • In this concept, organization is defined as the process of organizing works, people and the system.
    • It lays emphasis on individuals and considers organization as a continous pocess.

Characteristics of organization

  1. Division of work
  2. Co ordination
  3. Common Objective
  4. Co-operative Relationship
  5. Well defined Authority-Responsibility Relationship

Formal Organization

  • Refers to the structure of well defined jobs in a formally organized enterprise
  • Authority, Responsibility and accountability are fixed
  • Created deliberately
  • Created to achieve predetermined objective
  • Structure of well defined authority and responsibility
  • Formal Communication through official lines
  • Strict adherence to rules.

Informal Organization

  • It is the network of social groups based on friendships
  • Originates as a result of social interaction
  • Staisfy social and cultural needs
  • Structure based on human emotions and sentiments
  • Informal communication based on convinence
  • No formal rules exists.

Organizational Structure

  • Also known as Organogram, is a diagram or graphical representation of the relation between each position/officials in the organization.
  • It also shows the line of communication and heirarchy of the positions.
  • It consists of:-
    • Chain of command
    • Span of control
    • Communication line
    • Centralization and Decentralization
    • Formalization

Types of organizational structure

  1. Line Organization
    • Oldest and simplest form.
    • Line of authority flows vertically from farmost executive to lowest subordinates throughout the organization.
    • In this form managers have direct authority and responsibility over his subordinates
    • Merits:
      • Easy to establish
      • Clear defination of authority and responsibility
      • All activites realting to single department are managed by 1 individual
      • Every employee is clear whom he/she takes order from.
    • Demerits:
      • Line executives are generalist and specialist
      • Top level manager are overloaded with works
      • Concentration of power at top level, hence chance of failure when manager is not capable
      • Not suitable for large organization
  2. Line and Staff Organization
    • In this form we have two different types of authority:- line and staff authority
    • Staff authority means to advise, support and serve the line managers.
    • Merits:
      • Line executives are generalist and Staff executives are specialist and they work together in co-ordination
      • Top level manager are not overloaded with works
      • Chances of failure are less as even in case of incapable manager, staff executive can help them overcome the issues
      • Suitable for large organization
      • Better decision making and improvement in efficiency
    • Demerits:
      • It is diffcult to establish and is costly
      • Possibility of conflict between line managers and staff managers.
      • Allocation of authority and responsibility between line and staff official are not generally defined clearly
      • Reductin of initiative power of line authority
      • Line managers may be too dependent on staff managers and they are not responsible for any failures.
  3. Functional Organization
    • In this form people are grouped into departments of people with same skillset
    • Great for small organization with single product category as goal
    • Each team has people with mainly same skillset and a top level manager to deal with decision making, resources allocating ,etc
  4. Matrix Organization
    • In this form of organizational structure the functional group have lines of communication flowing both horizontal and vertical creating a matrix shape hence the matrix organization.
    • This type of structure is effective for quick demand response and supply.
    • Also for organization with dynamic work environment and multiple product.

Maslow’s hierarchy of Needs Theory

  • Maslow’s hierarchy of needs theory describes the needs of an human/employee from its owner in a hierarcal manner.
  • It suggests the needs of human is ever changing and once the need is fulfilled they move to another larger need.
  • In this theory the needs are categorized in:-
    • Lower Needs
      • Psycological Needs : Needs that are basic like JOB, salary, etc
      • Saftey Needs : After fulfiling the psycological needs they move to the saftey needs like job security, insurance plans, etc
    • Higher Needs
      • Social Needs : After all the lower needs are fulfilled they tend toward the first higher need which is social needs refering to Collegues, groups inside organization, etc
      • Esteem Needs : After social needs they go to esteem needs which might be salary increments, promotions, etc
      • Self actualization : After all those needs are fulfilled they need Self actualization which refers to them being recognized, their decisions being acknowledge.

Herzberg’s Two factor Theory of Motivation

  • Defined in 1959 by Herzberg also known as dual or motivation hygiene theiry is the theory of motivation that suggests that motivation can be created and preserved with two factors :- Hygiene factor and Motivational Factor.
  • Hygiene Factor are the job factors that doesnot create motivation but preserves it bt preventing dissatisfaction. Example may be pay, job security, physical working conditions, etc
  • Motivational Factor are the factors that yields the creation of motivation that is they leads to the satisfaction in doing the job. Example may be Recognition, Responsibikity, Growth, Job enlargment, etc
  • It has four states:-
    • High hygiene and High Motivation
    • High hygiene and Low Motivation
    • Low hygiene and High Motivation
    • Low hygiene and Low Motivation
  • The ideal state is the High Hygiene and High Motivation state

Expectancy Theory

  • Defined by VIctor H Vroom in 1964 and further extended by Porter & Lawler in 1968, this theory is based on the assumption that our behaviour is based on making of conscious choice from a set of possible alternative behaviours.
  • The behaviour we choose will always be the one that maximizes our pleasure and minimizes our pain.
  • It works in these 3 varaibles:-
    • Expectancy It tends that if you work hard you will eventually meet the target (i.e performance)
    • Instrumentality It tends to the behaviour of accessing for likely rewards they may receive doing the work.
    • Valence It tends to the perceived value of the reward by the employee (i.e the satisfaction level)
  • The formula for Motivational force = Expectancy * Instrumentality * Valence
